Business Memo



**To**: Customer Retention Team



**Subject**: Key Findings from Customer Churn Analysis



As part of the customer churn investigation, an exploratory analysis was conducted across customer, order, support-ticket, and engagement datasets. The goal was to understand customer behavior patterns and identify factors that may contribute to churn.



**Key Findings**



The analysis revealed several trends that appear to be associated with a higher likelihood of customer churn:



1\. Customers with lower engagement levels were more likely to stop purchasing.

2\. Frequent support issues appeared to be linked with increased churn risk.

3\. Customers with declining purchase activity showed stronger churn signals.

4\. Certain customer segments displayed noticeably higher churn rates than others.

5\. Customer experience indicators played an important role in retention outcomes.



**Recommendations**



Based on the findings, the following actions are recommended:



\- Focus retention efforts on customers showing early signs of disengagement.

\- Improve support response quality and issue resolution times.

\- Use targeted retention campaigns instead of offering discounts to all customers.

\- Continuously monitor customer activity to identify churn risk earlier.

\- Combine business knowledge with model predictions when making retention decisions.



**Next Steps**



Before launching any retention campaign, additional validation should be performed to measure the expected business impact and ensure resources are directed toward the most valuable customer segments.



**Conclusion**



The analysis provided useful insights into customer behavior and highlighted several areas where proactive intervention may help reduce churn and improve long-term customer retention.
